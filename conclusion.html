Conclusion

Ultimately, computers have had many different impacts on society and how we interact. There are various impacts, from how people have saved time by making calculations on computers, listening to music on mobile devices and the potential for time to be saved even more in the future with DNA and quantum computers. There has also been a profound impact with economics, with musicians now able to sell their music independently as well as the money saved by removing the need to solve problems with mathematicians, which often leads to these extra funds being placed into further computer research. On top of this, computers have changed how people interact with the world around them. People can hear new music and add it in seconds, as well as helping people in undeveloped countries solve maths equations despite being previously incapable in maths. For all of this progress to have occurred just about 30 years is evidence to how impactful computers have been on all aspects of society.
