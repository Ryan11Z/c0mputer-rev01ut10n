Science

How is scientific knowledge being used in computers?

Scientific knowledge has always been an important factor in the advancement of computer technology. It helped create some of the very first advancements by using materials such as silicon to make computing more and more compact. These advancements helped create many modern items that we take for granted now, such as smartphones and laptops. However, these technological advancements continue to occur today and into the future to advance computer technology as a whole.
One popular new scientific concept being used in computers is the idea of having a DNA computer. The DNA computer is computer filled with DNA encompassed with a liquid inside a container. They need to be inside a liquid so they can perform their computing. The whole idea of why DNA computer work is because DNA is made of two identical pieces. This enables any information on a piece of DNA to be split by chemicals, before being copied onto another random piece of DNA, transferring data around the computer much faster than in current PCs and other hand-held devices.

Image 1 - What a DNA computer looks like, with comparison to fingers beside it.

As shown in the image above, DNA computer chips are far smaller than currently used silicon chips. This is largely because a piece of DNA is tiny, and you only need the same number of pieces of DNA as you would in a silicon chip. However, there are some current issues with DNA computers before they could be used on a commercial scale. One issue with them is that they have not yet been proven outside of small tests and public stunts. Another issue with a DNA computer is the limited range of tasks that it can perform. This is because the code can only be given limited commands to perform, reducing the amount of possible uses for any DNA computer.

Image 2 - A close-up view of a quantum computer

What may be the biggest problem with DNA computers is not even the computers themselves, but the other technologies currently being developed. The main competition is in the form of quantum computers, which have had much more funding into research recently. This is largely due to fact that quantum computers perform much faster than DNA computers at the same size. This, combined with the constraints of DNA computers, have led to the rapid rise of quantum computers as a more viable and attractive concept. In summary, while DNA computers might still be useful in the future, alternatives are becoming available that are generally better in most aspects.
