Music

How have computers changed the music industry?

One feature of computers is their ability to replicate sounds. This concept is a very useful concept, especially in music. Computer power has enabled music to be created and re-created at a much quicker speed than previously. This has also led to digital music becoming the norm, with music being consumed in enormous amounts. This is a testament to how much computers and computer technology has transformed the ways music is created and consumed. 
The first step in digitally re-creating sound is to simulate the wavelength shape. However, there is a problem encountered in doing this because to properly simulate a wavelength, you need a large amount of memory and storage to copy exactly, wasting time and space on a computer or device. As a result, the wavelength is often rounded and compressed. This leads to a lower quality of music than an acoustic or analog sound. However, on the other hand, digitised music takes up much less physical space and can be used on a portable device.

Image 1 - Portable music players, such as the Ipod, have transformed the way in which music is consumed.

Despite the small size and convenience of using digital music, it didn't really kick off until 2001 with Apple releasing the very first iPod. Using this concept to store music, the device sold millions of items and music had become convenient. This led to a complete shift in how people could listen to music. Before, to hear music you either needed a radio or a CD player to listen to music at an acceptable quality. With the iPod, people could now listen to music during exercise, on public transport or even on an aeroplane. As a result of the increased availability to listen to music, more music was listened to by more people, increasing its popularity.

Image 2 - Music streaming services, such as Soundcloud and Spotify, have made free music streaming available to almost anybody.

Digital music has continued to evolve beyond the iPod to its current form. While you would previously need an iPod to play music, you only need a mobile phone and an app to play your music. Additionally, with music streaming services you can now play millions of songs for free. This has created an extremely free market, where any musician can upload their music to these sites and get it listened to by others. This enables musicians to gain fame and sell their music without the need for a record label to produce it.
Ultimately, the impact that computers have made in the music industry is profound. Despite the slight drawback in quality, digital music has become incredibly popular for people to use due to its portability and small size. Along with this, music has become freer to all with music streaming, enabling musicians to get heard without a record label. This has led to a breaking of norms in the whole music industry, giving everybody more freedom to choose what they listen to.
